Upgrade AYA lessons so that every lesson includes continuous conversation with the child.

Goal:
Children should feel that they are talking with AYA during the lesson, not just answering tasks.

Important:
Do NOT remove existing exercises.
Do NOT break homework solving.
Do NOT change the reward system.
Only extend lesson flow with interactive dialogue.

------------------------------------------------

Lesson conversation structure

Each lesson must include conversation between AYA and the child.

Lesson flow:

1 Greeting  
2 Lesson introduction  
3 Explanation  
4 Interactive question  
5 Exercise  
6 Feedback  
7 Encouragement  

------------------------------------------------

Example lesson interaction

AYA:

"Здравей! Днес ще учим умножение.

Готов ли си?"

Child interacts.

AYA continues:

"Чудесно!

3 по 4 означава да повторим числото 4 три пъти."

AYA asks:

"Колко е 3 × 4?"

Child answers.

AYA response if correct:

"Браво! Отлично се справи!"

AYA response if incorrect:

"Добър опит. Нека помислим още малко."

------------------------------------------------

AYA should frequently ask questions

Examples:

"Как мислиш?"
"Можеш ли да опиташ?"
"Искаш ли още една задача?"
"Готов ли си за следващото?"

------------------------------------------------

Add micro conversations

Between tasks AYA should speak briefly.

Example:

"Справяш се много добре!"
"Остава ни още една задача."

------------------------------------------------

Encouragement system

After correct answers AYA should use praise phrases:

"Браво!"
"Страхотно!"
"Много добре!"
"Ти се справяш отлично!"

The avatar can react with positive animation.

------------------------------------------------

Mini choices

Occasionally AYA should give options.

Example:

"Искаш ли по-лесна задача или по-трудна?"

Options:

Лесна  
Нормална  
Трудна  

------------------------------------------------

Voice friendliness

If voice mode is active, AYA should speak in short sentences suitable for children.

Avoid long paragraphs.
